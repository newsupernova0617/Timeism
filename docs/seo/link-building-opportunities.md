**Strategy:** Find broken links on relevant sites, propose SyncTime content as replacement.

**How it works:**
1. Identify relevant sites in network, event, or ticketing niches
2. Use broken link checker or Google Search to find dead links
3. Find SyncTime content that covers the same topic
4. Email site owner: "I found a broken link on your [page], here's a better resource"

## Link Quality Standards

### Only Accept Links From:

✅ **Topically Relevant Sites**
- Server/network/development/event industry content
- Related to timing, synchronization, ticketing, or performance
- Content that naturally references SyncTime's expertise

✅ **Sites with Real Traffic**
- Established publications (Dev.to, DigitalOcean, Eventbrite, Reddit)
- Visible domain authority (DA 30+)
- Real audience (not link farms or PBNs)

✅ **Contextual Links** (Highest Value)
- Link appears in article body where relevant
- Surrounded by related content
- Uses descriptive anchor text
- Natural, not forced

✅ **Diverse Link Profiles**
- Mix of industries (tech + event + education)
- Variety of link types (resource pages, guest posts, mentions, directories)
- Natural anchor text distribution

### Avoid:

❌ **Low-Quality Directories**
- Generic "add your link" directories
- Directories with no real traffic
- Directories in irrelevant categories

❌ **Comment Spam**
- Blog comment links (even if allowed, signals low effort)
- Forum signature links
- Unrelated blog comments

❌ **Paid Links**
- Violates Google policy
- "Sponsored link" exchanges
- Pay-to-list directories (unless established authority)
- Link schemes/networks

❌ **Link Farms**
- Sites with 100+ links per page
- Link exchange networks
- "PBN" (Private Blog Network) sites

❌ **Unrelated Sites**
- No topical relevance
- Random niches (e.g., casino, payday loans)
- Suspicious or spammy site profiles

### Quality Assessment Checklist

Before reaching out for links, verify:

- [ ] Site has real content (not just links)
- [ ] Site's audience overlaps with SyncTime users
- [ ] Site appears in Google search results for relevant keywords
- [ ] Site has no obvious spam signals
- [ ] Domain authority is at least 20 (low bar)
- [ ] Site has been active 6+ months
- [ ] Content is actually related to SyncTime's topic

## Appendix: Anchor Text Examples

### For Guest Posts
- "Read our complete guide to [topic]"
- "Learn more about [specific concept]"
- "See our in-depth analysis of [topic]"
- "Check out SyncTime's [resource]"

### For Broken Link Replacement
- "SyncTime's [topic] guide"
- "Complete [topic] resource"
- "Updated [topic] guide"

### For Directory Listings
- "SyncTime - [brief description]"
- "SyncTime server time tool"
- Contextual anchor text based on page content

### For Resource Pages
- "[Tool] for [use case]"
- "Best [category] tool"
- Natural description of resource

### Avoid
- "Click here"
- "Read more"
- "Link"
- "Resource"
- Generic non-descriptive text
